<p><p>Bifunctional chelates are compounds that contain two functional groups that can bind to metal ions. Inorganic chelates consist of metal ions bound to inorganic ligands, while organic chelates contain metal ions bound to organic ligands. Inorganic chelates are typically more stable and have higher binding affinities, making them popular in industrial and research applications. Organic chelates, on the other hand, are often more biocompatible and find use in biological and medical settings where compatibility with living systems is important.</p></p>
