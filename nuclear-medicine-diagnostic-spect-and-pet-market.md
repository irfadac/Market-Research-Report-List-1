<p><p>Nuclear medicine diagnostic (SPECT and PET) is a branch of medical imaging that uses radioactive substances to diagnose and treat diseases. Single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) and positron emission tomography (PET) are two commonly used techniques in nuclear medicine.</p><p>SPECT involves injecting a small amount of radioactive material into the patient's bloodstream, which emits gamma rays. These gamma rays are detected by a gamma camera, which creates 3D images of the patient's organs and tissues, helping to diagnose various diseases such as cancer, heart disorders, and neurological disorders.</p><p>PET, on the other hand, involves the injection of a small amount of radioactive material that emits positrons. These positrons interact with electrons in the patient's body, producing gamma rays. A PET scanner detects these gamma rays and creates detailed images of the patient's organs and tissues, providing valuable information for disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and monitoring.</p><p>The nuclear medicine diagnostic (SPECT and PET) market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
