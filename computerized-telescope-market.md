<p><p>The computerized telescope market presents three main types: refracting telescopes, reflector telescopes, and catadioptric telescopes. Refracting telescopes use lenses to gather and focus light, providing clear and high-quality images. Reflector telescopes, instead, use mirrors to capture light and are more affordable. Catadioptric telescopes integrate both lenses and mirrors, offering a combination of compactness and versatility. All three types benefit from computerized technology, allowing for accurate object tracking, automated data collection, and enhanced stargazing experiences.
