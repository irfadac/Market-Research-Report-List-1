<p><p>The municipal water treatment solutions market includes various types of technologies and processes. Reverse osmosis membranes, ion-exchange, ultrafiltration membranes, electrodeionization, biological filtration, chemical conditioning, clarification, filtration, chlorination, and others are key market segments. Reverse osmosis membranes use a semipermeable membrane to remove impurities. Ion-exchange involves replacing ions in water with ions from an exchange medium. Ultrafiltration membranes use small pores to filter out particles and microorganisms. Electrodeionization removes ions from water using ion-selective membranes. Biological filtration involves utilizing microorganisms to break down pollutants. Chemical conditioning includes adding chemicals to treat water. Clarification, filtration, and chlorination are traditional methods for removing impurities from water. Other solutions may include disinfection, ozonation, or advanced oxidation processes.</p></p>
