<p><p>The Telecom Energy Storage market consists of two main types: Stationary Energy Storage and Distributed Energy Storage. Stationary Energy Storage refers to large-scale storage systems that are designed to store excess energy generated by telecom networks for later use. This type of storage is typically located at a central location and is connected to the telecom grid. In contrast, Distributed Energy Storage involves smaller storage systems that are distributed across various telecom sites. These systems store and supply energy locally, ensuring a more efficient and reliable telecom network operation.</p></p>
