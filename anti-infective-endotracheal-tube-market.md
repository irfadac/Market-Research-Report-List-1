<p><p>An anti-infective endotracheal tube is a medical device used for intubation in patients who require ventilation support. These tubes are designed to reduce the risk of developing ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP) by incorporating antiseptic or antimicrobial agents into their surfaces. The anti-infective properties of these tubes help prevent colonization of bacteria, reducing the chances of infection.</p><p>The market for anti-infective endotracheal tubes is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and asthma, is driving the demand for intubation procedures. Additionally, the rise in hospital-acquired infections and the need for infection control measures in healthcare settings are fueling the adoption of anti-infective endotracheal tubes.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in medical technology, such as the development of tubes with enhanced antimicrobial coatings, are further propelling market growth. These coatings prevent the formation of biofilms and the growth of bacteria on the tube's surface.
