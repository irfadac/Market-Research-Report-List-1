<p><p>PET stands for Positron Emission Tomography, while CT stands for Computed Tomography. PET & CT systems combine the capabilities of both scans to provide detailed images of the internal structures and functions of the human body. PET scans use a radioactive tracer to detect changes in cellular activity, while CT scans use X-rays to create detailed images of the inside of the body.</p><p>The PET & CT systems market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 12.1% during the forecast period. One of the key factors driving this growth is the increasing prevalence of diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disorders, which require accurate diagnosis and monitoring. PET & CT systems help in the early detection of these conditions and aid in the personalized treatment planning of patients.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology have led to the development of hybrid PET & CT systems that offer improved diagnostic accuracy and reduced radiation exposure. These systems provide better anatomical and functional information, leading to more accurate diagnoses and treatment plans. Additionally, the increasing adoption of PET & CT systems by research organizations for drug development and preclinical imaging studies is also fueling market growth.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing demand for portable and compact PET & CT systems, especially in developing regions with limited access to healthcare facilities. These portable systems enable healthcare providers to perform scans in remote locations, ensuring timely and accurate diagnosis. Moreover, the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) in PET & CT systems is gaining traction, as it enables automated image analysis and improves diagnostic accuracy.</p><p>Overall, the PET & CT systems market is expected to witness substantial growth due to increasing disease prevalence, technological advancements, and the adoption of portable systems and AI integration. These trends are set to reshape the healthcare industry by delivering fast and accurate diagnoses, ultimately improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
