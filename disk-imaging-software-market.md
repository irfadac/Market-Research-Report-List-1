<p><p>Disk imaging software is a technology used for creating and storing exact copies of computer disks or drives. The market can be divided into two types: cloud-based and on-premise. Cloud-based disk imaging software operates on remote servers, allowing users to access and utilize the software features and store images via the internet. On the other hand, on-premise disk imaging software is installed and operated locally on a user's computer system. The choice between these market types depends on factors like data security, accessibility, and control preferences of the users.</p></p>
