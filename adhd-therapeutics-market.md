<p><p>ADHD therapeutics refer to medications and treatments used to manage Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) symptoms. Stimulants are the most common type of medication prescribed for ADHD, such as methylphenidate and amphetamines. They work by increasing the dopamine and norepinephrine levels in the brain, improving attention and reducing impulsivity. Non-stimulants, on the other hand, are an alternative type of medication for individuals who cannot tolerate or do not respond well to stimulants. They include atomoxetine and guanfacine, and they typically work by targeting norepinephrine levels in the brain.</p></p>
