<p><p>Interbody spinal implants are devices used in spinal fusion surgery to maintain spinal stability and promote the fusion of two or more vertebral bodies. These implants are inserted into the disc space between two vertebrae, replacing the damaged disc and facilitating fusion.</p><p>The interbody spinal implants market has been witnessing growth due to the increasing prevalence of spinal disorders, such as degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. The rising geriatric population, which is more prone to these conditions, is also contributing to market growth. Additionally, advancements in implant materials and design, such as the use of titanium and porous surfaces, have improved fusion rates and patient outcomes.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is driven by the growing demand for minimally invasive surgical procedures. Interbody spinal implants can be inserted through smaller incisions, resulting in reduced trauma, faster recovery, and minimized post-operative complications. This has led to a shift from traditional open surgeries to minimally invasive techniques, driving the adoption of interbody implants.</p><p>Other factors contributing to the growth of the interbody spinal implants market include the increasing number of orthopedic and neurosurgeons specializing in spinal surgeries, rising healthcare expenditure, and favorable reimbursement policies.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards the use of biologic materials, such as bone grafts and synthetic bone substitutes, to enhance fusion rates.
