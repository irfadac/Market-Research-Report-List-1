<p><p>Water electrolysis is a process that uses electricity to break water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen gas. It involves the use of an electrolyzer, which consists of two electrodes immersed in water and connected to a power source. When a direct electric current is applied, the water molecules split into their constituent elements: hydrogen gas is released at the cathode, and oxygen gas is released at the anode.</p><p>The future outlook for the water electrolysis market is quite promising. The rising demand for hydrogen as a clean and efficient source of energy is driving the growth of this market. <p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The water electrolysis market includes two major types of electrolyzers: traditional alkaline electrolyzer and PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) electrolyzer. Traditional alkaline electrolyzers use a solution of potassium hydroxide (KOH) as electrolyte and have been widely used for many years. PEM electrolyzers employ a solid polymer membrane as an electrolyte, which allows a higher working pressure and faster response time. They are becoming increasingly popular due to their higher energy efficiency, compact size, and ability to operate at low temperatures.</p></p>
