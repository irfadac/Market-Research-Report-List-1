<p><p>Emergency lighting control units refer to devices used to control and monitor emergency lighting systems in buildings. These units come in two broad types - wired and wireless. Wired control units require physical wiring connections to connect the control panel with the emergency lights, while wireless units utilize wireless technology to communicate between the control panel and lights. Wired systems are more traditional and typically employed in new constructions, while wireless systems offer more flexibility and convenience for retrofits or areas with limited accessibility for wiring installations. Both types serve the purpose of ensuring proper functioning and compliance with safety regulations.</p></p>
