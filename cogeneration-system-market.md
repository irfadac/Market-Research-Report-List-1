<p><p>Cogeneration is a highly efficient system that simultaneously produces electricity and usable heat using various technologies. The steam turbine cogeneration system uses steam to drive a turbine to generate electricity and heat. Gas turbine cogeneration utilizes the combustion of natural gas to generate electricity and recover waste heat. Fuel cell cogeneration combines hydrogen and oxygen to produce electricity and heat. Reciprocating engine cogeneration employs an internal combustion engine to provide both power and heat. Lastly, other cogeneration systems include technologies like Stirling engines, microturbines, and organic Rankine cycle systems, each with its unique characteristics.</p></p>
