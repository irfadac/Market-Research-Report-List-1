<p><p>Three-phase rectifiers are used in electrical systems to convert alternating current (AC) into direct current (DC). The market for three-phase rectifiers can be categorized into two types: half-wave rectification and full-wave rectification. In half-wave rectification, only one of the three phases is used to convert AC to DC, resulting in lower efficiency. In contrast, full-wave rectification utilizes all three phases, providing higher efficiency. While half-wave rectification is cheaper and simpler, full-wave rectification offers improved performance and is more commonly used in industrial and commercial applications.</p></p>
