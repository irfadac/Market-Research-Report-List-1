<p><p>The Ballistic Deflection Transistor (BDT) market is characterized by two main types: NPN and PNP transistors. NPN transistors are composed of three layers of semiconductor material, with two layers of negative (n-type) material sandwiching a positive (p-type) layer. They function by controlling the flow of electrons from the emitter to the collector, amplifying current. On the other hand, PNP transistors have the opposite configuration, with two positive layers sandwiching a negative layer. PNP transistors control the flow of holes (positive charge carriers) and work based on electron deflection.</p></p>
