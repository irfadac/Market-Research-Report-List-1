<p><p>Uncooled thermal imaging cameras are used to capture and visualize heat radiation emitted by objects. Short-wave IR cameras operate in the 1 to 3 µm range and are ideal for industrial applications. Mid-wave IR cameras, operating in the 3 to 5 µm range, are suitable for detecting gas leaks. Long-wave IR cameras, working in the 8 to 14 µm range, excel at monitoring electrical installations. Far-wave IR cameras operate in the 14 to 300 µm range and are primarily used in scientific research or military applications. They offer different benefits depending on the wavelength range in which they operate.</p></p>
