<p><p>Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) refer to a type of nuclear power reactor widely used in Western countries. These reactors use enriched uranium fuel and ordinary water as both the coolant and moderator. They operate at high pressure, preventing water from boiling. The market for PWR technology developed mainly in the Western world.</p><p>Soviet Pressurized Water Reactors (VVER) are a similar type of nuclear power reactor developed in the former Soviet Union and used in Eastern European countries. VVER reactors also use enriched uranium fuel and water as the coolant and moderator, but their design and construction vary from Western PWRs due to different technological and regulatory standards. The market for VVER reactors developed in the Soviet Union and its allied countries.</p></p>
