<p><p>The integrated circuit tag (IC label) market can be divided into two types: active and passive. In the active market, IC labels are equipped with a power source, allowing them to generate and transmit data independently. These tags are typically used in applications where constant monitoring or tracking is required. On the other hand, the passive market involves IC labels that do not have a power source of their own. These tags require an external power source, such as a reader or scanner, to activate and transmit data. Passive tags are commonly used in applications such as access control or inventory tracking.</p></p>
