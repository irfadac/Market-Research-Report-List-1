<p><p>Hereditary angioedema (HAE) is a rare genetic disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of swelling in various parts of the body, including the hands, feet, face, and airways. These episodes can be debilitating and potentially life-threatening. The treatment for HAE involves managing and preventing these attacks through medications, including prophylactic therapy and on-demand treatment.</p><p>Prophylactic therapy aims to prevent the swelling attacks, and it usually involves the use of drugs such as C1 esterase inhibitors, androgens, and plasma kallikrein inhibitors. On-demand treatment, on the other hand, focuses on providing immediate relief during acute attacks and often involves the administration of C1 esterase inhibitors, antifibrinolytics, or bradykinin receptor antagonists.</p><p>The market for hereditary angioedema treatment has experienced significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 12% during the forecast period.
