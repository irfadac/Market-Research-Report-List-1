<p><p>The embedded temperature sensor market consists of two main types: non-contact and contact temperature sensors. Non-contact temperature sensors are designed to measure temperature without physical contact with the object being measured. They use technologies such as infrared radiation or thermal imaging to detect and measure the temperature remotely. Contact temperature sensors, on the other hand, require direct physical contact with the object being measured. They can use methods like thermocouples, resistance temperature detectors (RTDs), or thermistors to measure temperature by detecting changes in electrical resistance. These two types offer different advantages and are used in various applications across industries.</p></p>
