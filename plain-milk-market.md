<p><p>The plain milk market can be divided into three main types: raw fresh milk, pasteurized milk, and normal temperature milk. Raw fresh milk refers to milk that has not undergone any heat treatment and is obtained directly from the source. Pasteurized milk is treated with heat to eliminate harmful bacteria while preserving its nutritional content. Normal temperature milk, also known as ultra-high temperature (UHT) milk, is heated to a higher temperature for a shorter period of time, extending its shelf life without refrigeration. These variations cater to different consumer preferences and requirements.</p></p>
