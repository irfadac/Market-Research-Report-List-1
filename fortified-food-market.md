<p><p>Fortified food refers to food products that have been enriched with additional nutrients that are not naturally present in the food or have been lost during processing. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Consumers are becoming more health-conscious and are seeking fortified food options to supplement their nutritional requirements.</p><p>Furthermore, changing lifestyles and a growing urban population have resulted in a shift towards convenience foods. Fortified food products provide a convenient way to meet nutritional needs. Additionally, an aging population and the rising prevalence of chronic diseases have also contributed to the demand for fortified food, as these individuals often require a higher intake of certain nutrients.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a surge in demand for organic and natural fortified food products. Consumers are placing more importance on organic and clean label food products, and manufacturers are responding by developing fortified food options that meet these preferences. Furthermore, the introduction of novel fortification techniques and ingredients, such as plant-based proteins and probiotics, is also driving market growth.</p><p>In conclusion, the fortified food market is experiencing steady growth due to increasing awareness of nutrition, changing lifestyles, and the demand for convenience.
