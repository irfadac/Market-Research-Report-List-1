<p><p>Orthopedic biomaterials are substances used in orthopedic surgeries to replace or repair damaged bone or cartilage. Metal orthopaedic biomaterials, such as stainless steel, titanium, or cobalt-chromium alloys, are strong and durable, providing stability and support to implanted devices. Non-metal orthopaedic biomaterials, like ceramics, polymers, or composite materials, offer advantages like biocompatibility, flexibility, and the ability to mimic natural tissue properties. Both types of biomaterials play a crucial role in the success of orthopedic surgeries and help improve patients' mobility and quality of life.</p></p>
