<p><p>Resistant Starch (RS) is a type of starch that resists digestion in the small intestine and reaches the colon intact. It is categorized into four types: RS1, RS2, RS3, and RS4. RS1 refers to physically inaccessible starch found in whole grains and seeds. RS2 includes starch that is resistant due to its high amylose content, found in uncooked potatoes and green bananas. RS3 denotes starch that forms during cooking and cooling processes in foods like cooked and cooled potatoes. RS4 represents chemically modified starches that are resistant to digestion.</p></p>
