<p><p>Hospital-acquired urinary tract infection (HA-UTI) testing refers to the diagnostic procedures and technologies used to detect urinary tract infections in patients who acquire the infection during their hospital stay. Thus, accurate and timely diagnosis of these infections is crucial for effective management and prevention.</p><p>The global hospital-acquired urinary tract infection testing market is anticipated to witness steady growth during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this growth, including the rise in the prevalence of HA-UTIs, the increasing aging population, and the growing awareness about hospital-acquired infections among healthcare professionals.</p><p>Technological advancements in diagnostic testing methods have also played a significant role in market growth. The introduction of rapid and automated testing techniques has enhanced the efficiency and accuracy of HA-UTI diagnosis. These advancements have led to improved patient outcomes and reduced healthcare costs by enabling early detection and targeted treatment.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing adoption of molecular diagnostic techniques, such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and nucleic acid amplification tests, has positively impacted market growth.
